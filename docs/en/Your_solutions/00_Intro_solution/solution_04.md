# Problem 4 — Rearranging Formulas

The period of a simple pendulum is given by

$$
T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}
$$

where

- $T$ — period of oscillation  
- $L$ — length of the pendulum  
- $g$ — acceleration due to gravity  
- $\pi$ — constant ($\approx 3.14159$)

We need to **rearrange the formula to make $g$ the subject**.

---

# Step 1 — Divide both sides by \(2\pi\)

$$
\frac{T}{2\pi} = \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}
$$

---

# Step 2 — Square both sides

$$
\left(\frac{T}{2\pi}\right)^2 = \frac{L}{g}
$$

---

# Step 3 — Multiply both sides by \(g\)

$$
g\left(\frac{T}{2\pi}\right)^2 = L
$$

---

# Step 4 — Solve for \(g\)

$$
g = \frac{L}{\left(\frac{T}{2\pi}\right)^2}
$$

---

# Step 5 — Simplify

$$
g = \frac{4\pi^2 L}{T^2}
$$

---

# Final Formula

$$
g = \frac{4\pi^2 L}{T^2}
$$
