# Problem 5 — Trigonometry (Vector Components)

We are given a vector:

$$
\vec{A} = 15
$$

and an angle with the horizontal:

$$
\theta = 60^\circ
$$

We are asked to find the **horizontal (x) and vertical (y) components** of the vector.

---

# Necessary Definitions and Formulas

For a vector $\vec{A}$ with magnitude $A$ and angle $\theta$ with the horizontal axis:

$$
A_x = A \cos\theta
$$

$$
A_y = A \sin\theta
$$

Where:

- $A_x$ — horizontal component  
- $A_y$ — vertical component  

---

# Step-by-Step Solution

## Step 1 — Horizontal Component

$$
A_x = A \cos\theta
$$

Substitute values:

$$
A_x = 15 \cos 60^\circ
$$

Recall:

$$
\cos 60^\circ = \frac{1}{2}
$$

So:

$$
A_x = 15 \cdot \frac{1}{2} = 7.5
$$

---

## Step 2 — Vertical Component

$$
A_y = A \sin\theta
$$

Substitute values:

$$
A_y = 15 \sin 60^\circ
$$

Recall:

$$
\sin 60^\circ = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \approx 0.866
$$

So:

$$
A_y = 15 \cdot 0.866 \approx 12.99
$$

---

# Final Answer

- Horizontal component:

$$
A_x = 7.5
$$

- Vertical component:

$$
A_y \approx 12.99
$$
