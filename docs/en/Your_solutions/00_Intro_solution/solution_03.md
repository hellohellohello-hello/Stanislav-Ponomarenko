# Problem 3 — Proportionality (Universal Law of Gravitation)

We are given the **Universal Law of Gravitation**:

$$
F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}
$$

where:

- $F$ — gravitational force between two masses  
- $G$ — gravitational constant  
- $m_1$, $m_2$ — masses of the two objects  
- $r$ — distance between their centers  

We must determine **how the force changes** if:

- the distance **$r$ is doubled**
- both masses **$m_1$ and $m_2$ are halved**

---

# Necessary Definitions and Formulas

## 1. Universal Law of Gravitation

The gravitational force between two masses is given by

$$
F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}
$$

This formula tells us that:

- Force is **directly proportional** to both masses.
- Force is **inversely proportional** to the square of the distance.

---

## 2. Direct Proportionality

If a quantity is directly proportional to another:

$$
F \propto m
$$

Then if the mass becomes **half**, the force becomes **half** as well.

---

## 3. Inverse Square Law

If a quantity follows an inverse square relationship:

$$
F \propto \frac{1}{r^2}
$$

If the distance doubles:

$$
r \rightarrow 2r
$$

then

$$
r^2 \rightarrow (2r)^2 = 4r^2
$$

Therefore the force becomes **four times smaller**.

---

# Step-by-Step Solution

We start with the original formula:

$$
F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}
$$

---

# Step 1 — Apply the Change in Masses

Both masses are halved:

$$
m_1 \rightarrow \frac{m_1}{2}
$$

$$
m_2 \rightarrow \frac{m_2}{2}
$$

Substitute into the formula:

$$
F' = G \frac{\left(\frac{m_1}{2}\right)\left(\frac{m_2}{2}\right)}{r^2}
$$

Multiply the masses:

$$
F' = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{4 r^2}
$$

So the force becomes

$$
\frac{1}{4}
$$

of the original due to the mass change.

---

# Step 2 — Apply the Change in Distance

The distance is doubled:

$$
r \rightarrow 2r
$$

Substitute into the formula:

$$
F'' = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{(2r)^2}
$$

Square the distance:

$$
(2r)^2 = 4r^2
$$

Thus

$$
F'' = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{4r^2}
$$

This makes the force **four times smaller** again.

---

# Step 3 — Combine Both Effects

From the masses we get a factor:

$$
\frac{1}{4}
$$

From the distance we get another factor:

$$
\frac{1}{4}
$$

Multiply the factors:

$$
\frac{1}{4} \times \frac{1}{4} = \frac{1}{16}
$$

---

# Final Result

The new force becomes

$$
F' = \frac{F}{16}
$$

---

# Interpretation

The gravitational force becomes **16 times smaller**.

The factor of change is therefore

$$
\frac{1}{16}
$$
