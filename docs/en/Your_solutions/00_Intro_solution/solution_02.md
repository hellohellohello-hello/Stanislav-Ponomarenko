# Problem 2 — System of Equations

We are given the following system of linear equations:

$$
2x + 3y = 12
$$

$$
x - y = 1
$$

Our goal is to find the values of **x** and **y** that satisfy both equations simultaneously.

---

# Necessary Definitions and Formulas

## 1. System of Linear Equations

A **system of equations** is a set of two or more equations that share the same variables.

The solution of the system is the set of variable values that satisfy **all equations at the same time**.

Example:

$$
\begin{cases}
ax + by = c \\
dx + ey = f
\end{cases}
$$

---

## 2. Substitution Method

The **substitution method** is a common way to solve systems of equations.

Steps:

1. Solve one equation for one variable.
2. Substitute that expression into the other equation.
3. Solve the resulting equation.
4. Substitute back to find the remaining variable.

---

# Step-by-Step Solution

We will use the **substitution method**.

---

# Step 1 — Solve the Second Equation for \(x\)

The second equation is

$$
x - y = 1
$$

Add \(y\) to both sides:

$$
x = y + 1
$$

Now we have an expression for **x** in terms of **y**.

---

# Step 2 — Substitute into the First Equation

The first equation is

$$
2x + 3y = 12
$$

Substitute \(x = y + 1\):

$$
2(y + 1) + 3y = 12
$$

---

# Step 3 — Expand the Equation

Multiply:

$$
2y + 2 + 3y = 12
$$

---

# Step 4 — Combine Like Terms

$$
5y + 2 = 12
$$

---

# Step 5 — Solve for \(y\)

Subtract 2 from both sides:

$$
5y = 10
$$

Divide by 5:

$$
y = 2
$$

---

# Step 6 — Find \(x\)

Recall:

$$
x = y + 1
$$

Substitute \(y = 2\):

$$
x = 2 + 1
$$

$$
x = 3
$$

---

# Step 7 — Verify the Solution

Check the values in the original equations.

### First equation

$$
2x + 3y
$$

Substitute:

$$
2(3) + 3(2)
$$

$$
6 + 6 = 12
$$

✔ Correct

---

### Second equation

$$
x - y
$$

Substitute:

$$
3 - 2 = 1
$$

✔ Correct

---

# Final Answer

The solution of the system is:

$$
x = 3
$$

$$
y = 2
$$

Or written as an ordered pair:

$$
(3, 2)
$$
